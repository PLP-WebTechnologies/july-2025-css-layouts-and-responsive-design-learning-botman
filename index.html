Overview

The Kenyan coastline is rich in biodiversity, home to coral reefs, mangroves, and numerous marine species in the Indian Ocean.

Marine Species

Coral Reefs

Coral reefs provide habitat for thousands of fish and invertebrates and help protect the coastline from erosion.

Sea Turtles

Green and hawksbill turtles nest on Kenya’s beaches, playing a vital role in the marine ecosystem.

Dolphins & Whales

Kenyan waters are a hotspot for dolphin and whale sightings, important for tourism and biodiversity.

Mangroves

Mangrove forests act as nurseries for juvenile fish and protect the coast from storms.

Conservation Efforts

Efforts include marine protected areas, community education, and sustainable fishing practices to preserve marine habitats.
